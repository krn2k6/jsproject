// 04 — improve

typing tips

01
home row position
Keep fingers resting on ASDF and JKL; — the home row. Your index fingers should feel the small bumps on F and J. This reduces movement and builds muscle memory.
02
look at the screen
Avoid looking at your keyboard. Train your fingers to find keys by touch alone. It feels slow at first, but this habit doubles your potential ceiling speed.
03
accuracy before speed
Slow down until you can type without errors. Speed will come naturally. Chasing fast WPM with low accuracy is counterproductive — mistakes add up fast.
04
relax your hands
Tension is the enemy of speed. Keep wrists level, fingers curved, and shoulders loose. If you feel strain, take a break — fatigue kills accuracy quickly.
05
consistent practice
15 minutes every day beats 2 hours once a week. Consistency builds the neural pathways that make typing feel effortless. Use a timer and make it a habit.
06
target weak keys
Notice which keys you always stumble on. Slow down on those specific letters. Drilling your weakest keys yields faster improvement than general practice alone.
